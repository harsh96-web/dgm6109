Workout Duration vs Sleep Hours: A 12-Day Training Analysis

Harsh Kumar — Lab #9, Plot 1

Hypothesis: As workout duration increases over time, sleep requirements also increase to support recovery. I expect that heavier training sessions (especially compound lifts like squats and deadlifts) demand longer sleep compared to lighter workouts. Protein intake should amplify this relationship, with higher protein supporting better restfulness at any sleep duration. This visualization uses Array.sort() to arrange data chronologically, revealing how my training intensity and recovery patterns evolved from September 25 through October 6.

Hover over any bubble to see workout details • Data sorted chronologically to show progression

Why This Visualization Works

This scatterplot effectively captures my 12-day fitness journey by showing four interconnected variables: workout duration (X-axis), sleep hours (Y-axis), protein intake (bubble size), and restfulness (bubble color). The chronological sorting reveals a clear upward trend — as I progressed from 50-minute moderate workouts to 102-minute heavy lifting sessions, my sleep needs increased from 6 hours to 8.5 hours. The color gradient (red to green) shows that restfulness improved alongside this progression, suggesting my body adapted positively to increased training loads.

What makes this particularly insightful is the protein dimension. Larger bubbles (higher protein intake) consistently appear at higher restfulness levels, validating my hypothesis that adequate protein supports better recovery. By hovering over any point, you can see the specific exercises performed (bench press weights, squat loads, etc.), directly addressing my professor's feedback about workout specificity. Without the chronological sorting via Array.sort(), this progression story would be invisible — just scattered data points rather than a clear fitness trajectory.
